## OSA Profile Keycaps Overview

### Introduction

Keycap profiles define the shape, height, and sculpting of keycaps on a mechanical keyboard, influencing typing comfort, sound, and visual appeal. Among the growing number of profile options, OSA occupies a distinctive position — a hybrid profile that bridges the gap between the widely familiar OEM shape and the tall, dramatic SA profile.

OSA was originally created by Artifact as a profile that merges the sculpted practicality of OEM with the elevated height and refined curves of SA. Keychron has since adopted the profile extensively, producing multiple OSA keycap sets and shipping it as the default on several keyboard lines including the Q series and V series.

### What is OSA Profile?

OSA is a medium-high sculpted keycap profile with a cylindrical top surface. It takes the OEM contour — the sculpted row layout most typists are already familiar with — and scales it up toward SA-like heights, creating a taller, more commanding presence on the board without reaching SA's extreme elevation.

Typical characteristics:

- **Overall height:** Approximately 11 mm — taller than Cherry (~9.4 mm), comparable to OEM (~11.9 mm), significantly shorter than SA (~16.5 mm)
- **Top surface:** Cylindrical (concave in one axis)
- **Sculpting:** Sculpted across rows, with each row at a different height and angle
- **Wall thickness:** 1.3–1.6 mm depending on variant
- **Material:** Double-shot PBT (primary), dye-sub PBT available in select colorways
- **Stem:** Standard Cherry MX (compatible with all MX and MX-clone switches)

### Design Characteristics

**Row sculpting.** OSA keycaps are sculpted across multiple rows, with each row at a distinct height and angle. The sculpting follows an OEM-like contour but with smoother, more pronounced curves. The top rows sit at steeper angles, the home row is relatively flat, and the bottom row tilts gently forward — guiding fingers naturally to the home position.

**Cylindrical top surface.** OSA uses a cylindrical top, curved in a single axis from front to back. This is the same approach used by OEM and Cherry profiles, and it provides a familiar resting surface for typists coming from prebuilt keyboards. The cylindrical shape offers a natural channel for fingertips without the deeper cupping of spherical profiles like SA or DSA.

**Ergonomics.** OSA's height sits in a comfortable middle range — tall enough to provide a satisfying sense of key depth and sculpted guidance, but short enough that a wrist rest is recommended rather than mandatory. The refined curves promote a natural hand position during extended use, and the cylindrical top provides stability and tolerance for slightly off-center keystrokes.

**Sound.** The combination of double-shot PBT construction at 1.3–1.6 mm wall thickness and medium-high height produces a deep, satisfying "thock." PBT's density contributes a lower-pitched, fuller sound compared to thin ABS keycaps. The acoustic signature is one of OSA's most praised qualities — deeper and more solid than OEM, without requiring the extreme height of SA or KSA to achieve it.

### Advantages

- **Balanced height.** OSA sits between the compact Cherry profile and the towering SA, offering the elevated feel of a taller keycap without the ergonomic demands of a full SA setup.
- **Familiar sculpting.** The OEM-derived contour means users transitioning from prebuilt keyboards adapt quickly. The sculpting feels natural to anyone who has typed on a standard keyboard.
- **Strong acoustics.** Double-shot PBT at 1.3–1.6 mm thickness delivers a rich, thocky sound that improves noticeably over stock OEM keycaps.
- **Durable construction.** Double-shot PBT resists shine, oil, and fingerprints. Legends are permanently molded into the keycap and will never fade or wear off.
- **Sustainable materials.** Select Keychron OSA sets contain at least 50% recycled PBT content.
- **Broad compatibility.** OSA sets cover ANSI layouts from 60% to 100% and fit any keyboard with standard Cherry MX stems — not limited to Keychron boards.
- **Multiple legend options.** Available in center-printed, side-printed (for backlight shine-through), and dye-sub variants.


### Comparison with Other Profiles

| Feature | OSA | OEM | Cherry | SA | KSA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Height** | Medium-high (~11 mm) | Medium-tall (~11.9 mm) | Medium (~9.4 mm) | Tall (~16.5 mm) | Tall (~16.5 mm) |
| **Sculpting** | Sculpted (smooth curves) | Sculpted (4 rows) | Sculpted (4 rows) | Sculpted (pronounced) | Sculpted (refined) |
| **Top surface** | Cylindrical | Cylindrical | Cylindrical | Spherical | Spherical (flattened) |
| **Material** | Double-shot PBT | ABS (typically) | PBT or ABS | ABS (typically) | Double-shot PBT |
| **Wall thickness** | 1.3–1.6 mm | Moderate to thin | Thick | Thick | 1.6 mm |
| **Sound** | Deep thock | Higher-pitched | Solid thock | Deep, resonant | Deep thock |
| **Wrist rest** | Recommended | Recommended | Optional | Required | Required |
| **Aftermarket** | Limited (Keychron) | Very wide | Very wide | Wide | Keychron primarily |
| **Best for** | Upgraded OEM feel, Keychron builds | Stock/prebuilt boards | Enthusiast daily drivers | Retro aesthetics | Retro feel with comfort |

**OSA vs. OEM:** The most natural comparison. OSA refines the OEM contour with smoother curves and upgrades the material from thin ABS to thick double-shot PBT. The height is similar, but the typing feel is noticeably more solid and the sound is significantly improved. OSA is essentially what OEM would be if it were designed for the enthusiast market.

**OSA vs. Cherry:** Cherry is shorter with a more compact silhouette. Both use cylindrical tops and sculpted rows, but Cherry sits closer to the desk and pairs with a wider range of aftermarket sets. Choose Cherry for a low-profile enthusiast experience with maximum customization options; choose OSA for a taller, thockier feel with an OEM-familiar contour.

**OSA vs. SA:** SA is substantially taller with spherical tops and deeper sculpting. SA delivers the most dramatic retro aesthetic and the deepest sound, but at the cost of requiring a wrist rest and a longer adjustment period. OSA offers a more moderate version of that experience — taller than Cherry, shorter than SA, with cylindrical rather than spherical tops.

**OSA vs. KSA:** Both are Keychron profiles, but they serve different preferences. KSA is taller (~16.5 mm) with a spherical top and flattened dish — closer to SA in philosophy. OSA is shorter with a cylindrical top — closer to OEM in philosophy. Choose KSA for the retro spherical experience; choose OSA for an elevated version of the familiar cylindrical feel.

### Common Use Cases

**Keychron Q and V series upgrades.** OSA ships as the default on many Keychron boards, and upgrading to a premium OSA colorway is one of the simplest ways to personalize the keyboard while maintaining the profile the board was designed around.

**Users upgrading from stock OEM.** Typists who are comfortable with OEM sculpting but want better build quality, sound, and aesthetics will find OSA the most natural upgrade path. The contour is familiar; the improvement in feel and acoustics is immediate.

**Extended typing and office work.** The sculpted rows and cylindrical tops provide reliable orientation for touch typists. The PBT surface resists shine during long daily sessions, and the thocky sound remains satisfying without being intrusive in shared workspaces.

**Gaming.** The sculpted rows help locate key clusters by feel, and the cylindrical tops provide a stable surface for rapid key presses. The medium-high height offers a good balance between key depth and finger travel speed.

**Sound-conscious builds.** Builders who want a deep, solid sound without committing to the height of SA or KSA will find OSA delivers strong acoustics in a more practical package.

### Summary

OSA is a refined, elevated take on the OEM profile — the same familiar sculpted contour, built to a higher standard with thick double-shot PBT and smoother curves. Choose OSA when you want an immediate upgrade over stock OEM keycaps without changing the fundamental feel of your typing experience. It delivers better sound, better durability, and a more polished look while keeping the cylindrical, sculpted design that most typists already know. The trade-offs are a limited ecosystem compared to Cherry profile and ANSI-only layout support, but for users within Keychron's keyboard lineup — or anyone looking for a thocky, well-built keycap in a proven contour — OSA is one of the most practical and satisfying profile choices available.
